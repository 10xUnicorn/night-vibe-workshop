SECTION 1: LOGO & VISUAL IDENTITY
================================================================================

Your app needs a logo. This is your first visual impression on users.

GENERATING YOUR LOGO WITH GEMINI
---------
Gemini can generate logos in seconds. Here's how:

STEP 1: Open Gemini
- Go to gemini.google.com
- Sign in with your Google account
- You'll see the chat interface

STEP 2: Write Your Logo Prompt
Create a detailed prompt describing your app's visual identity:

EXAMPLE PROMPTS:
   "Generate a modern, minimalist logo for a productivity app called TaskFlow.
   The logo should feature a checkmark and flowing lines. Use blue and white
   colors. Make it suitable for both light and dark backgrounds. Style: clean,
   professional, SaaS-ready."

   "Create a playful logo for a note-taking app called NotePath. Include a
   path or trail metaphor. Use warm colors (orange, yellow). The style should
   feel friendly and approachable. Make sure it works small (app icon size)."

KEY ELEMENTS TO INCLUDE IN YOUR PROMPT:
- App name
- What the app does (in one line)
- Visual metaphor or concept (checkmark, path, graph, etc.)
- Color preferences (2-3 main colors)
- Style (modern, playful, minimalist, bold, etc.)
- Intended use (app icon, website header, both)

STEP 3: Request Logo Options
- Paste your prompt into Gemini
- Ask for "3 different logo variations"
- Gemini will generate logo options

STEP 4: Refine Your Favorite
- Pick the variation you like best
- Ask Gemini to refine it: "I like option 2. Make the colors slightly more
  saturated and add more depth."
- Request specific changes until it's perfect

STEP 5: Download and Save
- Right-click the final logo
- Select "Save image as..."
- Save as PNG (keeps transparency) with a clear filename: logo_final.png
- Save multiple formats: PNG (web), JPG (social media), SVG (if available)

LOGO BEST PRACTICES
---------
✓ Keep it simple (should work at 16x16 pixels)
✓ Use 2-3 colors maximum
✓ Ensure it works on both light and dark backgrounds
✓ Make sure it's distinct from competitors
✓ Save multiple sizes: 64x64, 128x128, 256x256, 512x512
✓ Keep the original file organized in your project folder

WHERE TO USE YOUR LOGO
---------
- Top left of your website (links to home)
- Browser tab icon (favicon)
- Social media profile pictures
- Email signatures from your app
- Any marketing materials

SECTION 2: SOCIAL SHARE IMAGES
================================================================================

When users share your app on social media, you want a professional image to
appear alongside the link.

CREATING SHARE IMAGES WITH GEMINI
---------
Share images should be 1200x630 pixels (standard for social media preview)

IMAGE CONTENT IDEAS
---------
✓ App screenshot with headline overlay
✓ Logo with tagline on colored background
✓ Mockup of app interface with benefits listed
✓ Before/after (problem solved) visual
✓ Quote from early user testimonial

EXAMPLE GEMINI PROMPTS:
   "Create a social media share image for a productivity app called TaskFlow.
   Size: 1200x630. Include the app logo in the top left. Show a clean,
   modern app interface screenshot in the center. Add headline text: 'Get More
   Done Every Day.' Use a gradient background (blue to purple). Make it eye-
   catching and professional. Include small tagline at bottom: 'TaskFlow -
   Productivity Made Simple.'"

   "Design a launch social image for EmailBlast (email marketing tool). 1200
   x630. Show the app dashboard in the center. Add bold headline: 'Send 1000
   Emails in 60 Seconds.' Use bright colors (orange and white). Include
   testimonial at bottom: '3,500+ marketers trust EmailBlast.' Make it
   shareable and conversion-focused."

GEMINI WORKFLOW FOR IMAGES
---------
1. Describe exactly what you want in the image
2. Specify dimensions (1200x630)
3. Request visual style (modern, colorful, minimalist, etc.)
4. Ask for variations (light version, dark version, etc.)
5. Refine the best one
6. Download and save as PNG

SIZING FOR DIFFERENT PLATFORMS
---------
- Facebook & LinkedIn: 1200x630
- Twitter/X: 1200x675
- Instagram: 1080x1080 (square)
- Pinterest: 1000x1500
- Email: 600x300

CREATE MULTIPLE VERSIONS
---------
You need at least 3 versions:
1. Standard launch image (app + headline)
2. Alternative (different design, different message)
3. Mobile-friendly (taller, vertical format)

SECTION 3: ONE-LINER & APP DESCRIPTION
================================================================================

You need a perfect one-sentence description of your app. This appears
everywhere: App Store, landing page, social media bio, pitch decks.

CRAFTING THE ONE-LINER
---------
Formula: [App Name] is a [category] for [target audience] that [solves problem]

EXAMPLES:
   TaskFlow is a task management app for busy professionals that helps them
   organize their day and hit deadlines.

   EmailBlast is an email marketing platform for small businesses that lets
   them send professional campaigns without technical skills.

   NotePath is a note-taking app for students and researchers that organizes
   information by topics and connections.

CHARACTERISTICS OF GREAT ONE-LINERS
---------
✓ Under 20 words
✓ Clear problem/benefit
✓ Identifies target user
✓ No jargon (avoid: "leverages", "synergizes", "utilizes")
✓ Memorable
✓ Action-oriented

COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID
---------
✗ "TaskFlow is a comprehensive solution for organizational challenges"
   (Too vague, too corporate)
✗ "The #1 email platform in the world" (Unprovable, weak)
✗ "We leverage AI-driven synergies for digital transformation"
   (Corporate jargon, meaningless)

TESTING YOUR ONE-LINER
---------
Read it to someone who's never heard of your app. Do they immediately
understand:
1. What the app does?
2. Who it's for?
3. What problem it solves?

If not, it needs refinement.

WHERE YOUR ONE-LINER GOES
---------
- Website hero section (below headline)
- Social media bio
- Email signature
- Pitch deck introduction slide
- App Store description (first line)
- LinkedIn company description
- Any marketing materials

SECTION 7: MARKETING COPY WITH COPYLAUNCH.APP
================================================================================

Your landing page and marketing materials need compelling copy.
CopyLaunch.app helps you write conversion-focused copy quickly.

LANDING PAGE SECTIONS YOU NEED
---------
1. Hero Headline
   - Main benefit in one powerful sentence
   - Example: "Save 5 hours every week on email management"

2. Subheading
   - Expand on the benefit
   - Add a specific number if possible
   - Example: "EmailBlast does in seconds what used to take hours"

3. Hero Image/Screenshot
   - Show your app in action
   - Screenshot or mockup of dashboard

4. Pain Point Section
   - Acknowledge the problem
   - Show you understand user frustration
   - Example: "Tired of writing the same email 50 times?"

5. Solution Section
   - How your app solves the problem
   - Focus on results, not features
   - Example: "Create a template once, send to thousands instantly"

6. Features Section
   - 3-5 key features
   - Benefit of each (not just description)
   - Include screenshots or icons

7. Social Proof
   - User testimonials
   - Number of users
   - Case study or success story
   - Example: "Used by 500+ marketing teams"

8. Call to Action
   - Big, clear button
   - Specific action (Try Free, Get Started, Join Now)
   - Remove friction (no credit card required?)
   - Example: "Start free - no credit card needed"

USING COPYLAUNCH.APP
---------
1. Go to copylaunch.app
2. Describe your app and target audience
3. CopyLaunch generates copy suggestions for each section
4. Edit and customize for your voice
5. Test different headlines (which converts better?)
6. Add to your landing page

COPYWRITING PRINCIPLES
---------
✓ Focus on benefits, not features
   Bad: "Supports 50 integrations"
   Good: "Connect all your tools in seconds"

✓ Use power words
   Save, Increase, Discover, Eliminate, Unlock, Create, Achieve

✓ Specific numbers beat vague claims
   Bad: "Helps you work faster"
   Good: "Save 5 hours every week"

✓ Address objections
   "No credit card required" (removes risk)
   "Free forever plan" (lowers barrier)
   "30-day money back guarantee" (removes risk)

✓ Create urgency (without being pushy)
   "Limited spots for beta access"
   "Early founder pricing ends Friday"

SECTION 8: PRICING STRATEGY
================================================================================

How much should you charge? This depends on your app and market.

PRICING MODELS
---------
1. Free Forever Model
   - All features free
   - No ads, no limitations
   - Good for: building user base, B2B tools, utilities
   - Revenue: through premium features later, or sponsorships
   - Example: Notion, Discord, Figma (free tier)

2. Freemium Model (Recommended for Most Launches)
   - Free tier: limited features
   - Paid tier: unlimited or advanced features
   - Pricing: $5-20/month entry level
   - Good for: SaaS, productivity apps, content tools
   - Example: Slack, Dropbox, Notion
   - Pro: Lets users try free, easy upgrade

3. Tiered Pricing
   - Basic: $5-10/month (limited features)
   - Pro: $20-50/month (most features)
   - Enterprise: Custom pricing (all features)
   - Good for: complex apps, B2B software
   - Example: HubSpot, Zapier
   - Pro: Captures customers at different budgets

4. One-Time Purchase
   - Single payment, lifetime access
   - Price: $20-99
   - Good for: tools, utilities, templates
   - Pro: Simpler (no recurring billing)
   - Con: Harder to scale revenue

5. Usage-Based Pricing
   - Pay per action (per email sent, per API call, etc.)
   - Good for: tools with variable usage
   - Pro: Users only pay for what they use
   - Con: Can be unpredictable for users

PRICING PSYCHOLOGY
---------
✓ $9/month feels cheaper than $10/month (psychology of .99)
✓ Annual pricing ($99/year) feels cheaper than monthly ($9/month)
   even though it's similar cost
✓ Middle tier sells most (people avoid "cheapest")
✓ Show savings on annual plans ("-33% vs monthly")
✓ Include money-back guarantee (reduces risk)

RECOMMENDED FIRST LAUNCH STRATEGY
---------
1. Start with Freemium (free + $9-19/month)
2. Make free tier genuinely useful (so people try it)
3. Make paid tier obviously valuable (clear difference)
4. Check conversion rate after 3 months
5. Adjust pricing if needed

PRICING MISTAKES TO AVOID
---------
✗ Pricing too low (undervalues your work, hard to raise later)
✗ Pricing too high (nobody converts)
✗ Hidden fees (makes users angry)
✗ Changing pricing mid-use (users feel betrayed)
✗ Too many tiers (confuses buyers)

SECTION 10: NEXT STEPS AFTER WORKSHOP
================================================================================

Your launch is the beginning, not the end. Here's what comes next.

WEEK 1 AFTER LAUNCH
---------
□ Send launch email to all interested users
□ Post on social media (Instagram, LinkedIn, Twitter)
□ Launch on Product Hunt (if you choose to)
□ Post in communities (Unicorn Universe, Skool, etc.)
□ Respond to all feedback within 24 hours
□ Track metrics: signups, free-to-paid conversion, user engagement

WEEK 2-4 AFTER LAUNCH
---------
□ Collect user feedback systematically
□ Fix bugs and improve UX based on feedback
□ Build your first feature requests (from user feedback)
□ Start email newsletter if applicable
□ Create content about how to use your app
□ Reach out to 10 users for detailed feedback interviews

MONTH 2 AFTER LAUNCH
---------
□ Plan your "Version 1.1" (improvements based on feedback)
□ Build 2-3 small features users requested
□ Create case studies of happy users
□ Consider what metrics matter (DAU, conversion, retention)
□ Plan paid feature rollout if not done yet
□ Start growth experiment (referral program, partnerships, etc.)

ONGOING
---------
□ Monthly: Review metrics and adjust strategy
□ Weekly: Respond to user feedback
□ Monthly: Ship small improvements
□ Quarterly: Plan and build next big features
□ Always: Talk to your users (best insights come from conversations)

COMMON METRICS TO TRACK
---------
- Signups (how many new users)
- Activation rate (% of signups that use the app)
- Free-to-paid conversion (% of free users who upgrade)
- Churn rate (% of users who leave)
- Daily Active Users / Monthly Active Users (DAU/MAU)
- Retention (% of users still using after 1 month, 3 months, etc.)

WHAT YOU SHOULD NOT OPTIMIZE FOR YET
---------
✗ Revenue (focus on usage and retention first)
✗ Growth speed (focus on product quality)
✗ Number of features (focus on core value)
✗ User count (focus on happy users)

GETTING HELP AFTER LAUNCH
---------
- Claude can help with feature building and bug fixes
- Use Engineering Plugin for architecture questions
- Share user feedback with Claude to guide development
- Ask "What should I build next?" - Claude helps prioritize

HANDLING COMMON POST-LAUNCH CHALLENGES
---------
Challenge: Users love the app but don't upgrade
Solution: Make free tier more limited OR make paid tier more valuable

Challenge: Users churn after 2 weeks
Solution: Interview churned users to understand why, fix pain points

Challenge: No users signing up
Solution: Change messaging, target different audience, improve landing page

Challenge: Too much feedback, don't know what to build
Solution: Count requests, build most-requested features first

Challenge: Running out of time/energy
Solution: Focus on core features, get help, consider pricing to fund team
